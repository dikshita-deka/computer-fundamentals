# What is Firmware?

## Definition

Firmware is a special type of software that is permanently stored in hardware.
It is stored in non-volatile memory (memory that keeps data even when the power is off).

Firmware is stored inside a chip, but it is **not the chip itself**.
It is the **code or instructions** stored inside the chip.

---

## Key Characteristics of Firmware

- Firmware is a type of **low level software**.
- It is stored in **non-volatile memory** such as ROM or Flash memory.
- It is written and installed into hardware.
- It **starts automatically starts when the device powers on**.
- It helps hardware communicate with higher-level software like the operating system.

---

## Examples of Firmware

Common examples include:

- **BIOS**
- **UEFI**
- **Router Firmware**
- Firmware in devices like keyboards, Cameras and smart devices.

These pieces of firmware controls how the hardware behaves.

---

## Relationship with Hardware and Software

Firmware acts as a **bridge between hardware and software**.
Hardware needs firmware to start and operate properly.
Software depends on firmware to interact with the hardware.

Without firmware, most hardware devices would not know how to function when powered on.

---

## Cybersecurity Perspective

Firmware vulnerabilities can be very serious.
If attackers compromised firmware, they may gain deep control over a device.
Firmware attacks are difficult to detect because they operate at a very low level in the system.

That is why protecting firmware is an important part of cybersecurity.
